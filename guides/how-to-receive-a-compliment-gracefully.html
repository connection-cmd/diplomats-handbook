The Praise

How to receive a compliment gracefully

Someone says something kind, and we rush to bat it away — "oh, this old thing," "it was nothing." It feels modest. It's actually a small rejection of their kindness. Accepting praise well is a quiet, generous skill.

From The Diplomat's Handbook · a guide to graceful receiving

A compliment is a gift, and there's only one graceful thing to do with a gift: accept it. When you deflect, you're not being humble — you're gently overruling the other person's judgement and making them work to convince you. The warmer move is simply to receive it.

"Thank you" is a complete sentence

You don't need to earn a compliment or return one on the spot. A warm "Thank you — that really means a lot" honours both the words and the person who offered them. Meet their eyes, mean it, and let the moment be what it is.

Add warmth, not a rebuttal

  • A genuine note: "Thanks — I worked hard on that, so I appreciate you noticing."
  • Share the credit: "That's kind of you — the whole team put a lot in."
  • A touch of honesty: "Thank you. I was actually nervous about it, so that's lovely to hear."

Break the deflection habit

Notice your reflex — the "it was nothing," the instant subject change, the returned compliment fired back like a defence. None are wrong, exactly, but they crowd out the simpler, kinder response. Next time praise lands, resist the deflection, take a breath, and just say thank you. It feels strange once, and natural forever after.

Common questions

What's the best way to respond to a compliment?

A warm, simple "Thank you — that means a lot." You can add a genuine note, but you never need to earn or deflect it.

Why is it so hard to accept compliments?

We deflect out of modesty or discomfort — but brushing praise off can dismiss the other person's judgement. Accepting it warmly is more generous.

How do you accept a compliment without bragging?

Say thank you, then share credit or a small honest detail: "Thanks — the whole team put a lot into it." Warmth and humility do it.
