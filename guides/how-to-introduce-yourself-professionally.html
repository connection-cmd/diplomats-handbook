How to introduce yourself professionally

The first ten seconds set the tone. A crisp, warm introduction makes you easy to place and pleasant to meet; a mumbled or over-long one makes people work. Here's a simple formula that fits a meeting, an interview, a mixer, or an email.

From The Diplomat's Handbook · a guide to first impressions

A good introduction isn't about impressing — it's about orienting. You're giving the other person the few facts they need to place you and a reason to want to keep talking. Short, warm, and tailored to the moment beats a memorised speech every time.

The formula: name, role, hook

Almost every strong introduction has three beats — who you are, what you do, and a small hook aimed at the person or occasion. "Hi, I'm Maya — I lead design here, and I've been keen to hear how your launch went." The hook is what turns an introduction into a conversation.

Tailor it to the setting

  • A meeting: "I'm Tom from finance — I'll be supporting this project on the numbers side."
  • An interview: "Thanks for having me. I'm a marketer with five years in SaaS, and I really admire what your team's building."
  • Networking: "I'm Priya — I run a small studio doing brand design. What brings you here?"
  • Email: "Hi Sam, I'm Alex from the events team, reaching out about next month's conference."

Warmth carries it

Delivery matters as much as words. Make eye contact, offer a genuine smile, say your name clearly, and hand the conversation back with a question. You don't need to front-load your whole story — give just enough, show real interest in them, and let the rest unfold naturally.

Common questions

How do you introduce yourself professionally?

Name, what you do, and a relevant hook: "Hi, I'm Maya — I lead design, and I've been keen to hear how your project's going." Short and warm.

How do you introduce yourself in an interview?

A confident greeting, a one-line background, and genuine interest: "Thanks for having me. I'm a marketer with five years in SaaS…" Then let it open up.

How do you introduce yourself in an email?

Who you are and why you're writing, in the first line: "Hi Sam, I'm Alex from the events team, reaching out about the conference."
